# The Calendar
The planet makes one full rotation around the sun in three hundred and eighty days, marking one year.
The year is divided into thirteen month intervals of twenty-eight days. This in turn is divided into four weeks of seven days each.
The first day of each month is considered outside of the 28-day schedule and honors a different major deity.
However, the thirteen months only cover 377 days of the year. The final three days of the year are also considered outside the normal calendar, and are celebrated in a myriad of ways around the world. Usually they end up dedicated to more local deities and spirits, while in some more laid-back regions they mark a general period of rest, relaxation and contemplation.

The red moon, Sword, orbits the planet, completing a full orbit every fourteen days. The white moon, Shield, is much faster and completes its orbit in only five days.
Sword's orbit divides evenly into the 380-day year while Shield's does not. Every seven years both Sword and Shield are full on the first day of the year.
Every 200 days, eclipse season begins, lasting about three days. Sword Eclipses are somewhat rare but Shield eclipses are much more frequent, sometimes occurring multiple times in one eclipse season.
